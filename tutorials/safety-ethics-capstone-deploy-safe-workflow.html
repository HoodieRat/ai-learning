Safety Capstone: Deploy a Safe Workflow

You’ll turn safety into a deployable workflow: clear rules, safe data handling, logging with retention, and verification gates — without relying on vague reminders.

Prereqs

  • Safety & Ethics: Pitfalls (Data Leakage & Bias)

What you deploy

  • Rules: what data can be used and what can’t.
  • Workflow gates: when a human must review or approve.
  • Logging: what gets logged, what is redacted, and retention limits.
  • Verification: evidence required for high-stakes outputs.

Plan your deployment

  1. Choose one workflow (e.g., summarizing customer calls).
  2. Define inputs/outputs and which data is disallowed or redacted.
  3. Set review/verification gates: when a human must approve.
  4. Write logging + retention rules and who can access logs.

Mini build: test before launch

  1. Run a sample input; confirm redaction triggers and logs are sanitized.
  2. Test a negative/unsafe input; ensure the workflow blocks or escalates.
  3. Check monitoring signals (volume spikes, errors, declines) and alert paths.
  4. Document the post-launch checklist (who reviews, how often).

Practice Lab

Task: Deploy a safety-first workflow for a real use case.

Do: Choose one workflow (e.g., summarizing customer calls). Define inputs, outputs, redaction rules, review gates, and a monitoring plan (what to watch after launch).

Success criteria:

  • You define at least 3 review gates or verification steps.
  • You document logging and retention rules that minimize sensitive data.
  • You define at least 3 monitoring signals and what you do when they spike.

Verify: Run the workflow on a sample input and confirm the redaction and review gates trigger as designed.

Recap

  • Deploy with explicit data rules, review gates, and redaction/logging plans.
  • Test both happy path and unsafe inputs before launch.
  • Define monitoring and ownership so safety stays active after go-live.
