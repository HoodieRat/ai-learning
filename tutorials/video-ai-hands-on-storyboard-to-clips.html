Video AI: Hands-on Storyboard → Clips

You’ll turn a storyboard into clips while keeping continuity. The focus is an iteration loop that preserves identity and reduces flicker.

Prereqs

  • Video AI: Models & Pipelines

Workflow

  • Generate a keyframe per shot
  • Generate a short clip from the keyframe
  • Fix consistency issues before increasing length

Worked example: two matching clips

  1. Storyboard: subject in park, shot A sitting, shot B walking.
  2. Generate keyframes for A and B with same outfit/lighting.
  3. Create 3s clips from each keyframe; keep prompts identical except action.
  4. If identity drifts, regenerate with stronger reference or shorter clip.

Mini build: stabilize first

  1. Generate keyframes; lock identity and style.
  2. Produce 2–3s clips; inspect for identity/flicker.
  3. Fix issues (stronger refs, reduce motion) before making longer clips.
  4. Document which knob fixed stability for reuse.

Practice Lab

Task: Produce 2 clips that match the same subject identity.

Do: Keep the same identity constraints and vary only camera motion or action.

Success criteria:

  • Both clips clearly depict the same subject.
  • Continuity constraints are documented.
  • You can point to one knob that improved stability (reference, constraints, shorter clips).

Verify: Ask someone else if the two clips “belong together” without explanation.

Recap

  • Keyframes anchor identity; start with short clips.
  • Fix consistency before scaling length or complexity.
  • Document the settings that stabilized your clips.
