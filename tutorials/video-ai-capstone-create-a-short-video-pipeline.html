Video AI Capstone: Create a Short Video Pipeline

You’ll design a small pipeline that produces a short video reliably: planning, generation, stabilization, and assembly — with verification steps at each stage.

Prereqs

  • Video AI: Pitfalls
  • Image AI Capstone

Artifact

  • A 3-shot storyboard
  • Keyframes and clip settings
  • Final 10–20s assembled video

Plan your pipeline

  1. Write a 3-shot storyboard with consistency notes.
  2. Generate keyframes and lock identity/style.
  3. Choose clip lengths (short) and constraints (references, prompts).
  4. Define verification per stage: identity check, flicker check, assembly continuity.

Mini build: run and stabilize

  1. Create short clips for each shot; inspect for identity/flicker.
  2. Fix issues with stronger references or shorter duration before lengthening.
  3. Assemble the 3 clips; check transitions for jumps.
  4. Rerun one shot with a different action to prove modularity.

Practice Lab

Task: Produce a short (10–20s) final video.

Do: Keep it minimal: 3 shots, short clips, and a fixed identity/style guide.

Success criteria:

  • The three shots have consistent identity and style.
  • You can describe the pipeline stages and what you verify at each stage.
  • You can reproduce the pipeline to produce a second version.

Verify: Swap one shot’s action and rerun only that stage to confirm the pipeline is modular.

Recap

  • Short, constrained clips stabilize identity; assemble after fixes.
  • Verify each stage separately: keyframes, clips, transitions.
  • Modularity lets you rerun a shot without breaking the whole video.
