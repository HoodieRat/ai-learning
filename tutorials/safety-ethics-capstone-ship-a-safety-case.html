Safety & Ethics Capstone: Ship a Safety Case

You’ll create a lightweight “safety case” for an AI feature: what could go wrong, what mitigations exist, what you evaluate, and what you monitor after launch.

Prereqs

  • Safety & Ethics: Pitfalls
  • Evaluation: Hands-on Rubric + Judge

Artifact

  • Risk register (top risks + mitigations)
  • Evaluation plan (cases + pass criteria)
  • Monitoring plan (what to watch after launch)
  • Escalation path (who decides when to pause)

Plan your safety case

  1. Pick one AI-assisted workflow about to launch.
  2. List top 8 risks; map one mitigation per risk.
  3. Define evaluation: representative cases, pass bar, and who reviews.
  4. Set monitoring: 3–5 signals, thresholds, and on-call/escalation.

Mini build: write and review

  1. Draft the one-page safety case with the four sections above.
  2. Share with a teammate; ask them to find one missing risk.
  3. Add that risk and mitigation; tighten any vague language.
  4. Store the case with the runbook so operators can find it.

Practice Lab

Task: Produce a safety case for a real feature.

Do: Pick one AI-assisted workflow you might ship. Write a one-page safety case covering: risks, mitigations, evaluation, monitoring, and escalation.

Success criteria:

  • At least 5 risks are listed with concrete mitigations.
  • Evaluation includes representative cases and pass criteria.
  • Monitoring includes at least 3 signals and thresholds.

Verify: Have a colleague review the safety case and identify one missing risk; update accordingly.

Recap

  • Safety cases make risks and mitigations explicit before launch.
  • Evaluation and monitoring need owners and thresholds.
  • Peer review catches blind spots; keep the case alongside the runbook.
