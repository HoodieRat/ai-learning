Audio AI: Pitfalls (WER & Diarization)

Audio errors often hide in the parts that matter: names, numbers, and speaker attribution. This tutorial teaches you how to catch those failures reliably.

Prereqs

  • Audio AI: Hands-on Transcribe + Summarize

Failure modes

  • WER blind spots: low WER can still miss key entities.
  • Diarization drift: speaker labels swap mid-conversation.
  • Overlap: crosstalk causes missing or merged phrases.

Worked example: entity check

  1. Choose 5 critical entities (names, amounts, dates).
  2. Check transcript for each; mark errors by type (wrong value, missing, wrong speaker).
  3. If diarization drifts, relabel manually and note where it began.
  4. Capture a quick remediation note (e.g., tighten VAD, shorter segments).

Mini build: verification step

  1. List critical entities and speakers for your workflow.
  2. Create a 5-item checklist to verify them after transcription.
  3. Run on two clips; see which errors recur (overlap, label swap).
  4. Adjust the workflow (segment shorter, manual spot checks) based on findings.

Practice Lab

Task: Create a verification step for the “important bits”.

Do: Identify 5 entities that must be correct (names, dates, amounts). Check them in the transcript.

Success criteria:

  • You list 5 critical entities.
  • You confirm (or correct) them by inspecting the transcript.
  • You record which failure type happened (name error, diarization, overlap).

Verify: Re-run on a second audio clip and confirm your verification step still catches errors.

Recap

  • WER alone hides critical misses; verify entities and speakers directly.
  • Diarization drift and overlap need short segments and spot checks.
  • Keep a checklist to reuse on every transcription run.
