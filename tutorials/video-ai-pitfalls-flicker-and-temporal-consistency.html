Video AI: Pitfalls (Flicker & Temporal Consistency)

Flicker and identity drift aren’t random; they usually come from weak constraints or over-long generations. This tutorial teaches a diagnosis guide (as written criteria) and fixes.

Prereqs

  • Video AI: Hands-on Storyboard → Clips

Failure modes

  • Flicker: textures/lighting change frame-to-frame.
  • Identity drift: face/clothes morph over time.
  • Scene jumps: background discontinuities.

Fix strategies

  • Shorter clips + stitch (stability first).
  • Stronger reference constraints (keyframes, identity anchors).
  • Reduce degrees of freedom (don’t change subject + camera + environment at once).

Worked example: tame flicker

  1. Baseline: 6s clip with flicker.
  2. Change: regenerate as 3s with stronger reference frame and lower motion.
  3. Result: compare frames; flicker reduced, identity stable.
  4. Document the exact change (duration + reference) as the fix.

Mini build: diagnostic checklist

  1. Inspect a failing clip; classify issue: flicker, identity drift, scene jump.
  2. Apply one fix matched to the issue (shorter clip, stronger reference, fewer changes).
  3. Regenerate and compare side-by-side.
  4. Reuse the same fix on another clip to ensure it generalizes.

Practice Lab

Task: Fix flicker using one controlled change.

Do: Take a flickery clip and regenerate with one stronger constraint (reference frame, fewer prompt changes, shorter duration).

Success criteria:

  • The regenerated clip has noticeably less flicker.
  • You can explain which constraint reduced the variance.
  • You record the before/after plus your exact change.

Verify: Apply the same fix to a different clip and confirm it generalizes.

Recap

  • Classify the issue first; pick the fix that matches (shorter, stronger ref, fewer changes).
  • Short clips + references beat long blind generations.
  • Reuse your fixes across clips to build a stable recipe.
